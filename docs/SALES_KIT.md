# ProfitProof — first-customer sales kit

Everything here can be done from a couch with a phone. No walking required.
Tone rule for all of it: you're a **painter who built a tool for himself**, not a
software salesman. That's your unfair advantage — lead with it every time.

---

## Who to contact (make this list first — 10 names)

People you already know beat strangers 100 to 1. In order:

1. **Trades you've shared job sites with** — roofers, landscapers, gutter/window
   cleaners, pressure washers, electricians, plumbers.
2. **Businesses that have quoted you or referred you work.**
3. **Local business owners you know from suppliers, hockey, church, Facebook groups.**
4. PEI/Atlantic Canada local-service Facebook groups (post the "soft" version, below).

Ten names. That's the whole prospect list for week one.

## Objections you'll hear (and honest answers)

**"I already know where my jobs come from."**
> Maybe! Most of us *think* we do. The free 8 days either proves you right — nice —
> or shows you where money's leaking. Either answer is worth having.

**"I'm too busy for another tool."**
> That's the point of it — you don't run it, you read it. One dashboard, once a
> week. And I do the setup, not you.

**"$99/month is a lot."**
> One wasted ad channel costs more than that every month. If it can't find you at
> least that much waste in 30 days, you get every dollar back.

**"Is this some agency thing?"**
> No — I'm a painter. I built it for my own business because agencies kept selling
> me marketing nobody could measure. It's the opposite of an agency thing.

**"I don't use GoHighLevel/QuickBooks."**
> Then Starter isn't the right fit yet — the Done-for-you plan includes getting
> those set up, or honestly, come back when you're on them. (Say this! Turning
> away a bad fit earns trust that brings referrals.)

**"Let me think about it."**
> Totally fine. Only thing I'd say: the free week costs you nothing but a yes,
> and thinking about it costs another month of guessing. Offer stands either way.

## The 5-day couch plan

- **Day 1:** Write your 10-name list. Send Message 1 to the 3 you know best.
- **Day 2:** Send Message 1/2 to the next 4. Post the soft version in one FB group.
- **Day 3:** Rest day / answer replies. Anyone curious gets the one-pager.
- **Day 4:** Send to the last 3. Second FB group if you're up to it.
- **Day 5:** Follow-ups (Message 3) to non-repliers from Day 1–2.

**Expected math:** 10 personal contacts → 4–6 replies → 2–3 trials → **1 paying
customer** is a realistic first-week outcome. One Pro customer = $249/mo,
every month, recovering while you sleep. Three = $747/mo.

## When someone says YES

1. Reply: "Great — I'll set you up today. What's the best email for your login?"
2. Get them into the 8-day trial (once the app is deployed — see DEPLOY_RAILWAY.md —
   send them your live signup link; before then, book them honestly: "You're first
   in line, going live this week").
3. Put a reminder for **day 6 of their trial**: send them one screenshot of THEIR
   dashboard with one sentence: "This channel made you $X — this one made you $0."
   That screenshot is what converts trial → paid.
